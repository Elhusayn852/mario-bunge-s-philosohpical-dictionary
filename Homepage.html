About Mario Bunge

Mario Bunge (1919–2020) was a towering figure in contemporary philosophy, renowned for his rigorous defense of scientific realism and his systematic approach to the philosophy of science. An Argentine physicist and philosopher, Bunge authored over 80 books and 500 papers, most notably his monumental Treatise on Basic Philosophy, an eight-volume work that covers semantics, ontology, epistemology, ethics, and political philosophy. Passionate about clarity and logic, he championed a worldview grounded in scientific rationality and opposed relativism, mysticism, and pseudoscience. Bunge’s legacy lies in his commitment to unifying science and philosophy, making both more accessible, precise, and impactful in addressing the world’s pressing intellectual and practical challenges.
