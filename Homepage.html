Welcome to Mario Bunge's Philosophical Dictionary

The Philosophical Dictionary by Mario Bunge, now presented as an interactive website, offers a rigorous and accessible guide to modern Western philosophy. Reflecting Bunge’s humanist and scientistic perspective, the site combines clear definitions with critical essays that challenge academic conventions. Designed to be more than a static reference, it enables dynamic exploration through powerful search tools, contextual links, and multimedia features. This digital edition transforms Bunge’s lexicon into a living resource—an evolving space for philosophical inquiry, education, and debate.

Phenomenalism

When Appearances Replace Reality

Phenomenalism asserts that only phenomena—appearances to consciousness—are real or knowable. Bunge distinguishes between ontological and epistemological forms, both of which he critiques for denying the objective structure of reality. In science and everyday life, we routinely seek what lies beneath appearances, not merely the surface phenomena

Systemism

Everything Is a System—or Part of One

Systemism is Bunge’s favored ontological framework, asserting that every entity is either a system or a component of one. This worldview underpins his scientific realism and opposes both individualism and holism. It’s a call to see the world—and our knowledge of it—as structured, interconnected wholes

Paradigm Shift

How Worldviews Radically Change

A paradigm shift marks a fundamental change in a field’s background knowledge, hypotheses, and methods. It’s more than a scientific update—it’s a conceptual revolution. Bunge contrasts rational, evidence-based shifts with irrational conversions driven by fashion or ideology
